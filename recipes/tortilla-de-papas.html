Tortilla de Papas

Tortilla de papas is a traditional Argentinian take on the Spanish potato omelette. Made with simple ingredients; potatoes, eggs, and onions, it's a comforting and versatile dish enjoyed hot or cold, often served with bread or as part of a picnic or casual meal.

Ingredients

(4-6 Servings)
  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and thinly sliced
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 6 eggs
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Olive oil (for frying)

Steps

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
  2. Add the potatoes and onions. Cook gently, stirring occasionally, until soft but not browned (about 15-20 minutes).
  3. Remove the potatoes and onions with a slotted spoon and let them drain on paper towels.
  4. In a large bowl, beat the eggs. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked potatoes and onions to the eggs. Mix gently to combine.
  6. Heat a bit of oil in a nonstick skillet over medium heat.
  7. Pour the mixture into the skillet and cook on one side until mostly set (about 5-7 minutes).
  8. Carefully flip the tortilla (using a plate or lid to assist) and cook the other side for another 5 minutes.
  9. Slide onto a plate, let it rest for a few minutes, and serve warm or at room temperature.
